Section 01 / The Framework

The 8 pillars of client acquisition.

A complete system, broken down. Read in order. Each pillar builds on the last. Skip nothing.

01
Fix your positioning. This is where 90% of VAs lose.

Clients don't buy "Virtual Assistants." They buy specific results from a specific person for a specific kind of business. If your bio says "VA" — you're invisible.

✗ Generic
"I'm a Virtual Assistant"
✓ Positioned
"I help [business] get [result] using [skill]"
  • I help coaches book 10–15 calls/month using Instagram DMs
  • I help eCommerce brands increase sales through email campaigns
  • I help busy founders save 10+ hours/week with admin + automation
02
Optimize your profile to convert, not just look pretty.

Your profile is a billboard. Someone glancing at it for 5 seconds should be able to answer three things:

  1. Q1. Who do you help?
  2. Q2. What result do you deliver?
  3. Q3. What should they do next?
✓ Bio Template
Helping coaches book clients using DM outreach · VA · Lead Gen · Appointment Setter · 📩 DM "CLIENTS" to work with me
03
Post content that attracts clients — not just likes.

Likes don't pay rent. You need three content types working together:

A · Authority Content. Teach what you know. "How I booked 3 clients using DMs." "Why most VAs don't get replies (and how to fix it)."

B · Relatable Content. Be human. "Working with multiple clients is insane…" Builds trust without pitching.

C · Conversion Content. Case studies, results, soft offers. "Booked 5 calls in 3 days using this script. Want it? Comment 'SCRIPT'."

04
DM outreach is where the money is actually made.

Posting alone won't bring consistent clients unless you go viral every week. The real lever is starting conversations daily with the right people. The simple flow:

1. Warm engagement. Like 2–3 of their posts. Leave one real comment.

2. Send the DM. Not salesy. Just natural. "Hey [Name], saw your post about [topic] — that was actually solid. Curious, are you currently doing your own outreach or have someone helping you?"

3. Continue the conversation. Ask about their business. Identify pain points. Don't pitch yet.

4. Soft pitch. "I actually help with that — want me to show you what I'd do for your setup?"

05
Hunt where your clients actually live.

Not every platform fits every client. Pick where your ideal client already spends time. We break this down by platform in the next section, but the rough map:

  • Instagram — coaches, creators, lifestyle brands
  • LinkedIn — agencies, founders, B2B services
  • Facebook Groups — local businesses, niche communities, gold for cold leads
  • TikTok — newer brands, creators, younger audiences
06
Use lead magnets to skip the "hire me" awkwardness.

Instead of leading with "I'm available, hire me," lead with value. Trade something useful for a conversation. The framing shift is everything.

✗ Pushy
"Hire me as your VA"
✓ Magnetic
"I'll send you the DM script that books me calls"

Lead magnets build trust, conversations, and authority — in that order.

07
Most of your money is in the follow-up.

People rarely reply on the first message. They're busy. They forgot. They're lurking. The follow-up sequence:

DAY 2 · "Hey, just bumping this in case it got buried 👍"
DAY 4 · "Quick idea I had for your page to get more inquiries — want me to send it?"
DAY 7 · "All good if not the right time, just let me know 👍"

Three follow-ups. Spaced. Not pushy. This is where most clients come from — not the first message.

08
Run the daily system. Consistency > perfection.

One viral post won't save you. A daily system will. You need a repeatable rhythm. We map this out in the Daily System section below — but here's the headline:

  • 10–20 targeted engagements (likes, comments, story replies)
  • 10–15 personalized DMs
  • 1 value post
  • 5 follow-ups on existing conversations

Do this daily for 14 days and your inbox changes. Do it for 60 and your business changes.

Section 02 / Platform Playbook

Where your clients actually are.

Each platform has its own gravity. Pick one. Master it. Then expand. Trying to dominate all four at once is how VAs burn out.

Facebook
Group Gold

Underrated and overflowing with leads — especially in private niche groups where your ideal clients are already asking questions out loud.

  • Join 5–10 groups where your niche hangs out (coaching, eCom, agencies)
  • Answer questions with real value — never pitch in the comment
  • Move warm leads to DM after they engage with your reply
  • Post case studies in your own feed for credibility
Instagram
Reach + DMs

Best for coaches, creators, and lifestyle brands. Your DM game lives or dies here. Reels for reach, stories for trust, DMs for closing.

  • Bio that converts in 5 seconds (positioning + CTA)
  • 3 Reels/week — pick one content pillar, repeat it
  • Story polls + question stickers to surface warm leads
  • Reply to every comment — that's a free DM opener
LinkedIn
B2B Engine

If your client is an agency owner, founder, or operator — this is the highest-ticket platform. Less noise, longer trust cycle, bigger contracts.

  • Headline = your offer, not your title (avoid "Virtual Assistant")
  • Post 3x/week — insights, lessons, behind-the-scenes
  • Connect + comment for 14 days before you DM anyone
  • Voice notes in DMs — almost no one uses them, all stand out
TikTok
Reach Cheat Code

Fastest organic reach in the game right now. Less polished, more authentic. Good for VAs willing to show their face and teach.

  • Talking-head videos teaching one tip per video (60–90 sec)
  • Hook in the first 2 seconds — or you're skipped
  • Pin a "start here" video that explains who you help
  • Drive clients to Instagram or email — TikTok DMs are weak
Section 03 / DM Script Library

The exact scripts that get replies.

Niche example: online fitness coaches. Same structure works for any niche — swap the pain points and you're done.

7

The full sequence — first message to close.

Seven steps. Each one designed to feel human, not scripted. Volume + quality. If you do 15–25 of these per day, your pipeline fills up within two weeks.

STEP 01/ Opener
First Message · After engaging with their content
"Hey [Name], saw your post about [fat loss / client transformation] — that was solid. Quick question — are you currently getting clients mostly from content or are you doing outreach too?"
Why it worksNot salesy. Question is about THEM. Easy to reply with one word.
STEP 02/ Discovery
If they reply — go deeper, don't pitch
"Got it. Are you getting consistent inquiries weekly or is it kind of up and down?"
"How many calls are you booking per week right now?"
GoalGet them talking. Numbers especially. The more they share, the warmer the lead gets.
STEP 03/ Identify the gap
Mirror their pain back to them — without being condescending
"Yeah, I see that a lot — especially with coaches relying only on posting. Most of the ones I talk to aren't maximizing DMs or follow-ups, which is where most clients actually come from."
Why it worksPositions you as someone who understands the pattern. Builds authority without bragging.
STEP 04/ Soft pitch
Offer to help — frame it as a tweak, not a sales pitch
"I actually help coaches with that — mainly filling their pipeline through targeted outreach + booking calls. Want me to show you what I'd tweak in your current setup?"
Key phrase"Want me to show you what I'd tweak" — low commitment, high curiosity.
STEP 05/ Mini value drop
If they say YES — give 3 quick wins
"Cool — quick look at your page, here's what I'd fix:
– You've got solid content, but no clear CTA to start conversations
– You're probably leaving money in DMs (no follow-up system)
– You're not reaching out to warm leads (people already engaging)

Fixing just those can bring in more calls without more posting."
Why it worksDemonstrates competence. Plants the idea that they need help. Doesn't ask for the sale yet.
STEP 06/ Close (low pressure)
Make the ask small. No big commitments.
"If you want, I can handle this side for you so you can focus on coaching + closing. We can start small — just helping you book [X] extra calls per week. Want to try it out for a week and see results?"
Key"Try it for a week" reduces resistance. Easy yes.
STEP 07/ Follow-ups
Three messages, spaced over a week
DAY 2 · "Hey bro, just bumping this in case it got buried 👍"
DAY 4 · "Quick idea I had for your page to get more inquiries — want me to send it?"
DAY 7 · "All good if not the right time, just let me know 👍"
TruthMost replies come on day 4 or 7. Stop quitting after day 1.

Section 05 / The Daily Loop

Your daily client-getting machine.

2–4 hours, done daily. Run this loop for 14 days before judging it. Most VAs quit on day 5.

Block 01 · 30–45 min
Find Leads
Search IG, FB groups, LinkedIn. Look for: posting consistently, selling something, low engagement (= they need help).
Block 02 · 30 min
Warm Up
Like 2–3 posts. Reply to a story. Leave a meaningful comment. Makes your DM feel natural, not cold.
Block 03 · 1–2 hrs
Send DMs
Goal: 15–25 DMs/day. Don't overthink each one — volume + quality balance is the unlock.
Block 04 · 30 min
Post Content
One post. One pillar. Builds authority so your DMs convert easier next week. Repeat the same theme.
Block 05 · 15–30 min
Follow Up
Critical block. Most clients come from follow-ups, not first messages. Never skip this.
Section 06 / Avoid these

Mistakes that kill your replies.

If you're doing any of these — stop today.

×
"Hi I'm a VA, do you need help?"Generic, lazy, instantly ignored. Lead with their problem, not your title.
×
Pitching on the first messageYou haven't earned the right yet. Ask. Listen. Diagnose. Then pitch.
×
Writing long paragraphsIf your DM looks like an email, it gets closed. Short. Conversational. Human.
×
Sending random cold messagesIf you can't define your niche in one sentence, neither can your prospect.
×
No follow-up systemOne message, then ghosting them yourself? You're leaving 70% of clients on the table.
×
Sounding desperate"Please consider me!" energy repels clients. Calm confidence converts.
